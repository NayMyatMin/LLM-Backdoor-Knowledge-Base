# Input-Aware Dynamic Backdoor Attack

## Authors
Anh Nguyen, Anh Tran

## Venue
NeurIPS 2020

## Year
2020

## URL
https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.08138

## Abstract Summary
This paper introduces a novel backdoor attack paradigm where the trigger pattern is dynamically generated based on each input, rather than using a fixed universal trigger pattern. The authors propose an input-aware attack that leverages a trigger generator network to produce sample-specific triggers, making the attack significantly harder to detect by existing defense mechanisms that rely on identifying a common trigger pattern across poisoned samples.

## Key Contributions

1. **Input-aware dynamic trigger generation**: Proposed the first backdoor attack where the trigger pattern varies per input, generated by a learned trigger generator network conditioned on the input image. This fundamentally challenges defenses that assume a fixed or input-agnostic trigger.

2. **Diversity loss for trigger variation**: Introduced a diversity loss term that encourages the trigger generator to produce diverse patterns across different inputs, preventing the generator from collapsing to a single fixed trigger and ensuring genuine input-dependence.

3. **Evasion of existing defenses**: Demonstrated that the dynamic nature of the triggers makes the attack robust against multiple state-of-the-art defenses including Neural Cleanse, STRIP, SentiNet, and Spectral Signatures, which all assume some form of trigger universality.

4. **Novel training pipeline**: Developed a two-stage training pipeline where the trigger generator and the backdoored classifier are jointly optimized, with careful balancing between clean accuracy, attack success rate, and trigger diversity.

## Method Details
The attack consists of two main components: (1) a trigger generator network G that takes a clean input x and produces a sample-specific trigger pattern t = G(x), and (2) the backdoored classifier f that is trained on both clean and poisoned samples. The poisoned sample is created by applying the generated trigger to the input: x' = (1 - m) * x + m * t, where m is a mask controlling trigger transparency.

The training objective combines three losses:
- **Classification loss**: Standard cross-entropy on both clean and poisoned samples to maintain clean accuracy while ensuring attack success.
- **Diversity loss**: Encourages different triggers for different inputs by maximizing the distance between triggers generated for different samples. This is implemented as a negative cosine similarity between trigger patterns of different inputs.
- **Smoothness loss**: Regularizes the trigger pattern to be spatially smooth and visually inconspicuous.

The generator is trained end-to-end with the classifier using alternating optimization. The trigger patterns are constrained to be small in magnitude (controlled by the mask m) to maintain visual imperceptibility.

## Key Results
- Achieves attack success rates above 95% on CIFAR-10 and GTSRB while maintaining clean accuracy within 1% of clean baseline models.
- Successfully evades Neural Cleanse detection: the anomaly index remains below the detection threshold (2.0) for all target classes.
- Bypasses STRIP defense: the entropy distribution of poisoned inputs overlaps significantly with that of clean inputs, preventing detection.
- Defeats Spectral Signatures: the spectral signature of poisoned samples is indistinguishable from clean samples because the trigger pattern varies across inputs.
- The diversity loss is shown to be critical through ablation studies; without it, the generator collapses to near-fixed patterns that become detectable.
- Visual inspection shows triggers are imperceptible and vary meaningfully across different input images.
